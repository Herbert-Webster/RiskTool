Calculation Methodology

This appendix documents every calculation performed by the 3Ls Investment Planner — how values are derived, how weighted averages are constructed, and how the data is presented in the summary panel, bucket cards, and Excel export.

1. Bucket Allocations Liquidity · Lifestyle · Legacy

Each bucket receives a percentage of the total portfolio. The monetary value of each bucket is calculated as:

Bucket Value (£) = Total Portfolio (£) × Bucket % ÷ 100

Allocations can be entered either as a percentage (moving the slider) or as a monetary amount in the £ input field — the tool converts between the two automatically. If you enter a £ amount, it back-calculates the percentage as:

Bucket % = Entered Amount (£) ÷ Total Portfolio (£) × 100

The Unallocated pill shows any portfolio value not yet assigned to a bucket:

Unallocated (£) = Total Portfolio (£) − Liquidity (£) − Lifestyle (£) − Legacy (£)
2. Individual Holding Values

Each holding within a bucket can be entered as either a % of bucket or a £ value — the two fields are linked and update each other in real time:

Holding Value (£) = Bucket Value (£) × % of Bucket ÷ 100
% of Bucket = Holding Value (£) ÷ Bucket Value (£) × 100

When the total portfolio value or a bucket percentage is adjusted, £ values are preserved (frozen) and the % of bucket fields are back-calculated to reflect the new bucket total. This ensures amounts already entered are not silently overwritten.

3. Bucket Allocation Progress Bar

The progress bar within each bucket shows how much of the bucket's total value has been allocated to holdings:

Used % = Σ (% of Bucket) across all holdings in that bucket

The status states are determined as follows:

ConditionStatusColour
No holdings / £0 bucket—Neutral
Used % within 0.05% of 100%✓ Fully allocatedTeal / green
Used % > 100% (by more than 0.05%)⚠ Over by X%Red
Used % < 100%£X remaining (Y%)Muted

A 0.05% tolerance is applied around 100% to absorb floating-point rounding that arises when percentages are derived from £ values.

4. Ongoing Charges Figure (OCF)

OCF is calculated per holding and then summed for each bucket and the total portfolio. The weighted OCF % for a bucket uses each holding's % of bucket as its weight:

OCF (£) per holding = Holding Value (£) × OCF % ÷ 100
Bucket Weighted OCF % = Σ (% of Bucket × OCF %) ÷ Σ (% of Bucket)
Total Portfolio OCF (£) = Σ OCF (£) across all holdings in all buckets
Total Portfolio OCF % = Total OCF (£) ÷ Total Portfolio (£) × 100

This methodology matches the original Herbert & Webster spreadsheet.

5. Yield

Yield is calculated identically to OCF — per holding, then weighted and summed at bucket and portfolio level:

Yield (£) per holding = Holding Value (£) × Yield % ÷ 100
Bucket Weighted Yield % = Σ (% of Bucket × Yield %) ÷ Σ (% of Bucket)
6. Risk Score (Weighted Average)

Risk scores run from 0 (lowest) to 5 (highest). The weighted average risk uses each holding's £ value as its weight — ensuring larger positions have proportionally greater influence on the overall score:

Weighted Risk = Σ (Holding Value £ × Risk Score) ÷ Σ (Holding Values £)

This is calculated at both bucket level (shown as the pip meter on each bucket card) and at portfolio level (shown in the summary panel and Firm View).

A simple unweighted average would divide the sum of risk scores by the number of holdings — this is deliberately not used, as it ignores the fact that larger holdings represent a greater proportion of the client's exposure.

Holdings with a risk score of 0 are treated as a legitimate rating (e.g. cash or money market funds) and are included in the weighted average. A holding assigned 0 will pull the weighted average downward proportionally to its value.
7. Risk Pip Meter Display

The six rectangular pips (0–5) are filled up to the rounded integer of the weighted average risk score — this determines which pips are lit, and the colour of those pips:

PipColourInterpretation
0White / neutralNo measurable risk

The numeric score displayed alongside the meter shows the precise weighted average to two decimal places — the pip display is rounded for visual clarity only.

8. Summary Panel

The summary panel at the bottom of the planner consolidates all figures:

FieldHow it is calculated

UnallocatedTotal Portfolio minus the sum of all three bucket values
Total OCFSum of OCF £ across every holding in all buckets; expressed as both £ and % of total portfolio
Donut chartVisual split of Liquidity / Lifestyle / Legacy bucket values as a proportion of total portfolio
9. Allocation Warning (Save & Export)

When saving a client record or exporting to Excel, the tool checks whether any bucket that contains holdings is not fully allocated. A bucket is considered fully allocated if its used percentage is within 0.05% of 100%. Buckets with no holdings are skipped. If issues are found, a modal presents:

Issue typeShown as
Under-allocated£X remaining · Y% unallocated (gold)
Over-allocated+Y% over · £X excess (red)

The user may proceed anyway or go back and correct the allocation.

10. Firm View

The Firm View aggregates data across all saved client records in browser storage, plus the current unsaved session if it contains data. All monetary figures, weighted averages, and top-10 rankings are derived from the same formulas as above, applied across the combined dataset:

MetricMethod
Total AUMSum of total portfolio values across all clients
Total OCFSum of all individual holding OCF £ values across all clients
Weighted RiskValue-weighted average of risk scores across all holdings from all clients
Top 10 fundsHoldings grouped by fund name, summed by £ value, ranked descending
